After conducting a thorough review of Mass Landscaping Inc., we are pleased to submit this Letter of Intent to acquire 100% of the assets of Mass Landscape Inc. In structuring our LOI we have taken into account the following:

  • 2024 Adjusted SDE of $693,275 based on the recast provided in the CIM (tax return source)

  • Loyal recurring maintenance customer base with 5+ year average retention and strong commercial snow contracts, with low employee turnover rate
03

Deal Structure

The purpose of this letter is to outline the manner in which Methodic Ventures LLC, and/or Assigns (“Buyer”), proposes to acquire the Company (“Seller”). The parties recognize that the transaction will require further documentation and approvals, including the preparation and approval of one or more formal agreements setting forth the terms and conditions of the proposed acquisition (the Purchase Agreement). Nonetheless, this letter contains the parties’ non-binding expression of interest in negotiating the terms of a Purchase Agreement consistent with this letter.

Transaction Structure. The Buyer will purchase from the Seller all the assets, tangible and intangible, owned or used by the Seller in connection with the conduct of its business (“Business”), collectively the “Assets.” The Assets will include, without limitation:

  • All furniture, fixtures, and equipment (FF&E) as listed in the equipment schedule (estimated $1,500,000 in value)
  • All inventory (estimated $110,000–$120,000 in value)
  • All customer relationships, contracts, and goodwill
  • All website assets, brand identity, and intellectual property
  • All of the Seller’s contracts and agreements with contractors, vendors, or customers
  • Working capital sufficient to operate the business at close — the exact amount will be determined during due diligence based on seasonal cash needs and accounts receivable/payable cycles
  • Any other assets as both Parties may identify in due diligence

Except as listed above, the Buyer will not assume any other liabilities or obligations of the Seller except to the extent otherwise hereafter agreed between the parties. The Seller shall be responsible for all liabilities of the Seller which are not assumed by the Buyer. All liabilities for which Seller is responsible and which are known at the time of closing shall be paid from the portion of the Purchase Price paid by the Buyer at the closing, and the Seller will indemnify and hold harmless the Buyer against all liabilities and obligations of the Seller which are not assumed by the Buyer.

The final structure of the transaction is subject to legal, accounting, and tax review by each party and each such party’s legal and accounting advisors.

04

Consideration

Payment for the Assets will consist of a total purchase price of $1,500,000, which is comprised as follows:

  • Cash at Close: $150,000 (10% of purchase price)
  • Seller Financing: $1,350,000 (90% of purchase price) — The Seller will carry a note for $1,350,000 at 6% annual interest on the outstanding principal balance, repaid from operating cash flow as described below.

Seller Financing Repayment Terms

The seller note of $1,350,000 carries a 6% annual interest rate on the outstanding principal balance, repaid from operating cash flow as follows:

  • Base Annual Payment: $100,000 per year from operating cash flow.
  • Cash Flow Participation: In addition to the base payment, the Seller will receive 30% of all operating cash flow.

Cash distributions to the Seller are based on our projections of a modest 5% increase year-over-year in distributable cash flow. The estimated repayment schedule is as follows:

05

Transition Period

The Seller will provide twelve (12) months of full-time transition assistance following close, continuing to perform daily operational duties including estimating, crew oversight, customer relationship management, and vendor coordination. This extended transition ensures continuity and allows the new operator to fully absorb the institutional knowledge that has made this business successful.

The Seller’s compensation during the transition period is provided through the seller financing payments described above (estimated ~$253,544 in Year 1). This structure directly ties the Seller’s income to business performance, creating alignment between both parties during the critical first year.

Following the 12-month transition, the Seller will be available for on-call consulting for the remaining duration of the seller note at no additional cost (estimated 2–4 hours per month for questions, introductions, or strategic guidance).

Standard non-compete and non-solicitation provisions will apply in the Buyer’s primary geography for the duration of the seller financing term.

06

Facility

The Buyer proposes to lease the current Seller-owned facility for a minimum of thirty-six (36) months post-close at the current fair market rate ($2,000–$4,000/month as referenced in the CIM), with the rate fixed for the initial 36-month term. The Seller will cooperate fully in securing a permanent location of 15,000–20,000 square feet if the Buyer elects to relocate within the first year.

07

Next Steps

Given the need for further discovery, we propose a sixty (60) day period of exclusivity so that we may undergo a formal due diligence process and bring in any appropriate third parties to assist with finalizing the deal. It is our intention to close faster than the 60-day period, but we are requesting this extra time in case anything unexpected arises. During this time, we will conduct our due diligence while working with the Seller to complete a Purchase Agreement. Time is of the essence during this due diligence period.

Confidentiality

In consideration of the obligations under this Agreement, the parties agree that this Letter of Intent, and its terms, and any confidential information disclosed by either party through discussion and other communication arising from, or regarding this Agreement, are strictly confidential, and that the parties (or their agents, attorneys, legal representatives and assigns, collectively “Their Agents”) shall not disclose or disseminate any information concerning any such terms to any third person(s).

Should either Buyer or Seller disclose this information to Their Agents through the course of business, or concerning this agreement, the Buyer or Seller agree to take responsibility for notifying Their Agents of the confidential terms agreed to herein, and their responsibilities to remain confidential. Further, Buyer and Seller agree that neither shall make any announcements to third parties related to this transaction other than as mutually agreed in writing as specified by terms of the Agreement, and that until the time that such agreement exists, no announcements will be made. In the event that the Agreement never becomes final, and is not executed by the parties, then this confidentiality provision shall remain in effect for two years from the date of disclosure.

This LOI is non-binding except for the confidentiality and exclusivity provisions above. We look forward to working together to finalize a mutually beneficial agreement and to preserving the legacy you have built at Mass Landscape Inc.
